Georgia

Georgia is a state in Eurasia, the Caucasus, on the eastern coast of the Black Sea. It is bordered by Russia to the north, Turkey and Armenia to the south, and Azerbaijan to the southeast. Georgia is located at the crossroads of Southeast Europe and Western Asia, but it is part of Europe sociopolitically and culturally. Georgia is a country of ancient civilization. This area was inhabited by humans as early as 1.7 - 1.8 million years ago. This fact is justified by the skull of Homo erectus (a healthy person) and the lower jaw of an archantrope, discovered in 1999 in the territory of Dmanisi, in the south-east of Georgia. Today, the person from Dmanisi is considered the oldest inhabitant of the Eurasian continent and is even called the "first European". An important trade route passed through the territory of Georgia, known as the "Silk Road". This road connected India and Central Asia with Europe. On the one hand, it contributed to the socio-political and economic growth of the country, and on the other hand, due to its favorable strategic location, it repeatedly became an object of attack by the invaders, fell under the influence of others, lost territories. Christianity prevailed in Georgia from the early 4th century; Imperial Russia partially conquered Georgian lands from 1801 to 1866, and from 1922 it became part of the Soviet Union. After a chaotic period caused by the civil war and severe economic crisis, the country became relatively stable in the late 1990s. Georgia gained independence again in 1991. In August 2008, Russia again attacked Georgia and occupied the territories of Upper Abkhazia and the former South Ossetia that covered Georgia's jurisdiction. Currently, in the official documentation of international organizations and various countries, Russia is referred to as an occupier. Currently, Georgia is a member of several international organizations, including the United Nations, the Council of Europe, GUAM, the World Trade Organization and the Black Sea Economic Cooperation. Georgia also aspires to European integration and membership in the North Atlantic Alliance. Georgia is an ancient, centuries-old and rich country of culture, which is indicated by cultural monuments and various works of art created in ancient times. Georgia is distinguished by a variety of architectural structures that have become the country's treasures. In large and small towns and villages, there are many architectural temples of a unique style, with intricate details and striking frescoes. Churches and monasteries built in the first millennium have survived to this day, which indicates that construction technology was highly developed in Georgia. An important part of Georgian culture is folk music, which is unique worldwide. Georgian folklore is characterized by polyphony, which is the pinnacle of folk musical thought. It is no coincidence that in 2001, UNESCO recognized the Georgian polyphonic song as a World Intangible Cultural Monument and included it in the list of cultural heritage. The capital of Georgia is Tbilisi, which is located in eastern Georgia. In particular, in the Tbilisi cavern on both banks of the Mtkvari River at an altitude of 380-600 meters above sea level, its area is 504.8 km2. According to legend, the territory of Tbilisi was covered with forests, and King Vakhtang Gorgasali often hunted in these forests. It was during one of the hunts that the king was wounded by a deer, which bathed in a hot spring, recovered and fled. According to another version, the king's mimino is a pheasant. Both of them were in a hot spring. Due to the hot springs and the favorable location of this place, the king was deforested and the city was founded. The main artery of Tbilisi is the Mtkvari River, which runs 35 kilometers through the city. The Mtkvari River within Tbilisi has several tributaries both left and right. There are two lakes (Lisi Lake and Turtle Lake) and one reservoir in Samgori, which is known as the Tbilisi Sea. Both lakes are quite popular recreational areas.
